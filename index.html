LINKIN PARK

ABOUT

Linkin Park is an American rock band from Agoura Hills, California. Formed in 1996, the band rose to international fame with their debut album, "Hybrid Theory" (2000), which was certified Diamond by the RIAA and multi-Platinum in several other countries.

The band's music incorporates elements of alternative rock, nu-metal, rap rock, and electronic music, often characterized by their powerful vocals, memorable melodies, and innovative use of samples and electronic effects.

Over the years, Linkin Park has released several critically acclaimed albums, including "Meteora" (2003), "Minutes to Midnight" (2007), and "A Thousand Suns" (2010), exploring diverse themes and musical styles while maintaining their signature sound.

In addition to their commercial success, Linkin Park has been praised for their philanthropic efforts and activism, including their support for various charitable organizations and causes, such as disaster relief, mental health awareness, and environmental conservation.
